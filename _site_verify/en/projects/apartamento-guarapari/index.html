Guarapari Apartment

São Paulo, Brazil — 2023

Specifications

  • Location São Paulo, Brazil
  • Year 2023
  • Area 125 m²
  • Architects Arquipélago Arquitetos, Pianca Arquitetura
  • Photographs Pedro Kok
  • Manufacturers Selvvva

The renovation of a 1970s apartment in São Paulo aimed to adapt it to the needs of a young family. The original 1972 floor plan by architect Ruy Ohtake was recovered and received specific updates to exacerbate the programmatic aspects of this modernist home.

The materiality of the spaces received a new proposal. The curved wall, wrapping the living and dining areas, was detached from the ceiling and finished in tauari wood panels, with a discreet pass-through opening to the kitchen. The amber film on the windows was maintained, bathing the living spaces in a soft warm light, amplified by the satin white planes of the wooden furniture and custom anodized aluminum elements, such as the suspended linear light and dining table.

A white concrete bench runs along the living room and office, reappearing at the bar. A rough texture was applied to the ceiling, recalling the domestic ambience of São Paulo architecture from the original period. The kitchen and laundry feature lush stainless steel countertops and light-toned cabinetry, contrasting with the rest of the apartment. In the intimate balcony, accessible only from the bedrooms, pink ceramic tiles and smooth counters create a playful space. These elements form welcoming spaces that filter and attenuate the pulse of the bustling metropolis outside.
